# Cassandra thesis-chapter card

Purpose: place Cassandra inside the Tyche evidence-infrastructure thesis without merging it into adjacent projects, and keep structural observation separate from legal status, signature validation, and supervision.

## Chapter role

Cassandra is the governance-artifact observatory chapter. It shows how public legal-technical state can be transformed into durable evidence infrastructure: observed, hashed, bundled, signed, timestamped, displayed, and independently checked while legal interpretation remains outside the system boundary.

## Chapter question

How can public machine-readable governance artifacts become reproducible evidence packages and public telemetry without pretending to be legal supervision or relying-party validation?

## Chapter argument

1. Public governance artifacts are already machine-readable enough to observe longitudinally.
2. Observation becomes research evidence only when the pipeline preserves provenance, hashes, manifests, diffs, and caveats.
3. Evidence packaging and dashboard display are powerful but dangerous unless the claim boundary is explicit.
4. Synthetic fixtures are necessary because live public observations alone do not prove edge-case behavior.
5. The Cassandra pattern generalizes to AI governance records as evidence infrastructure, not as automated legal approval.
