# Cassandra press-safe summary

## Safe short summary

Cassandra is a public research observatory that studies how machine-readable governance artifacts can be turned into reproducible evidence packages. It collects dated public artifacts, records hashes and provenance, compares structural changes, packages evidence, verifies package receipts, and publishes aggregate dashboard telemetry with explicit caveats.

## Safe quote

"Cassandra is about evidence integrity, not legal interpretation: it shows how public governance artifacts can be observed, hashed, bundled, verified at the package level, and discussed without claiming to supervise or validate their legal effect."

## Safe bullets

- Public-source, research-only observation of governance artifacts.
- Dated manifests, hashes, structural diffs, and evidence packages.
- EATF/AEP receipts verify package bytes and declared hashes.
- Dashboard shows aggregate telemetry and caveats.
- Synthetic fixtures test edge behavior without provider-specific claims.

## Avoid saying

- That Cassandra validates trusted lists or source signatures.
- That Cassandra determines legal status or compliance.
- That Cassandra is a public alerting or supervisory service.
- That zero observed structural diff means no legally relevant change.
- That EATF receipts prove the real-world meaning of the underlying artifacts.
