# Cassandra evidence infrastructure manifest

Date: 2026-05-27
Status: thesis-facing manifesto and operating statement

## One sentence

Cassandra: from governance infrastructure to evidence infrastructure.

## Position

PKI is governance infrastructure. It is not only cryptography. It is the institutional arrangement by which public authority, private operators, software, audits, lists, policies, and relying parties coordinate trust.

Cassandra does not replace that governance infrastructure. Cassandra watches a public part of it and turns the observation process itself into evidence infrastructure.

## What Cassandra demonstrates

1. Public legal-technical artifacts can be observed without pretending to interpret legal status.
2. Dated observations can become reproducible evidence objects: manifests, hashes, diffs, bundles, receipts, schemas, dashboard cards.
3. EATF/AEP can verify evidence package integrity without claiming the underlying governance artifact is legally valid.
4. MIRROR-style bundles can make claims, sources, caveats, and local verification readable by humans.
5. Synthetic fixtures are not a weakness; they are how the case proves behavior without exposing sensitive or named-entity claims.
6. A live dashboard is not marketing. It is a public method surface: it shows what the evidence stream contains and what it refuses to claim.

## What Cassandra refuses

Cassandra refuses to be:

- a trusted-list legal validator;
- a source-signature validator for relying-party use;
- a supervisor;
- a compliance engine;
- a public alerting service;
- a proof that no legally relevant change occurred;
- a provider-specific status oracle.

This refusal is part of the contribution. Evidence infrastructure is powerful only when it knows where its evidence stops.

## Thesis fit

The thesis should use Cassandra as the public-governance-artifact case. The other cases should sit around it:

- MIRROR: human-reviewable evidence bundles.
- EATF/eatf.eu: package-level attestation framework.
- Janus: AI-assisted multilingual review evidence.
- MATx: AI decision-level replay and tamper evidence.
- Vesta: public web/citation drift evidence.
- Icarus: reproducibility and artifact-claim evidence.
- eaudit and h2oatlas: public professional/environmental information cases, only where current public facts support them.
- Aletheia AI and avatar.eatf.eu: prototype/partner-integration attempts, cited only after publicness and permission checks.

## Research statement

The turning point is institutional, not only technical. eIDAS, ETSI, RFC, NIST, CA/B Forum, PKIC, W3C, OpenID, and ISO already provide much of the older machinery of digital trust. The AI governance problem is that evidence must now follow systems, models, decisions, public records, translations, datasets, and reviews over time.

Cassandra makes that problem small enough to inspect and real enough to publish.
