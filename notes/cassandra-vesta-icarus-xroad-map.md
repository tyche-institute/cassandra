# Cassandra / Vesta / Icarus / X-Road map

Purpose: distinguish evidence-infrastructure cases without merging their corpora or claims.

| Case | Evidence surface | What becomes observable | Cassandra relationship | Separation rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cassandra | Public LOTL-derived trusted-list artifacts | Dated structural telemetry, hashes, diffs, bundles, EATF package receipts, dashboard index | Governance-artifact observatory anchor | Does not determine legal status or validate source signatures. |
| Vesta | Public web pages, citations, and drift surfaces | Citation drift, content persistence, source-change evidence | Parallel public-record drift case | Does not reuse Cassandra eIDAS corpus or provider/service handles. |
| Icarus | Reproducibility artifacts and audit trails | Reproducibility evidence, run traces, audit checkpoints | Parallel reproducibility-audit case | Does not turn Cassandra dashboard counts into reproducibility scores. |
| X-Road agent attestation | Operational public-sector trust infrastructure and agent attestation records | Attestation evidence, operational trust boundaries, public-sector service context | Comparative infrastructure-governance case | Separate legal/operational context; no Cassandra trusted-list claims transferred. |

## Cross-case thesis pattern

All four cases share a method pattern: preserve source/provenance, hash artifacts, package evidence, expose verifier-readable outputs, and keep the semantic/legal boundary visible.

## Cassandra-specific caution

Cassandra remains aggregate, structural, and research-only. It should not be used as the empirical corpus for Vesta, Icarus, or X-Road, and those cases should not be cited as proof that Cassandra performs legal interpretation, relying-party validation, public alerting, or supervision.
